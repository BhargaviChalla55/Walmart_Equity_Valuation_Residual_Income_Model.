# Walmart_Equity_Valuation_Residual_Income_Model.
**Project Overview:**

This project presents an equity valuation of **Walmart Inc. (NYSE: WMT)** using the **Feltham–Ohlson Residual Income Model**. The analysis evaluates Walmart’s intrinsic equity value based on profitability, risk, capital efficiency, and long-term growth assumptions.

**Objective**

To estimate Walmart’s intrinsic stock value and assess whether the current market price reflects the company’s fundamental financial performance.

**Data Sources**

- SEC 10-K Reports
- Yahoo Finance
- U.S. 10-Year Treasury Rate
- Walmart Annual Report
- Market Risk Premium estimates

**Valuation Method**

- Feltham–Ohlson Residual Income Model
- Cost of Equity (CAPM)
- Abnormal Earnings Forecast
- Sensitivity Analysis
- Terminal Growth Assumptions

**Key Assumptions**

- Risk-Free Rate: 4.23%
- Market Risk Premium: 2.74%
- Beta: 0.69
- Cost of Equity: 6.12%
- ROE: 21.41%
- Dividend Payout Ratio: 34.4%
- Terminal Growth Rate: 2%–4%

**Scope & Analysis**

- Business and industry analysis
- Cost of equity estimation
- Abnormal earnings calculation
- 7-year and extended forecast horizons
- Sensitivity analysis on growth and horizon
- Comparison of intrinsic value vs market price

**Key Findings**

- Walmart generates returns above its cost of equity
- Low beta indicates lower market risk
- Intrinsic value is close to the current stock price
- Longer forecast horizons increase estimated equity value
- Walmart remains a stable long-term investment.

- Full valuation analysis.
